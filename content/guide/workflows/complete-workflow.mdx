# Complete Application Workflow

A step-by-step guide for applying to a specific job.

## Phase 1: Preparation

### Analyze the Job Posting
1. **Read the full posting** twice
2. **Highlight keywords** — skills, technologies, requirements
3. **Note the company values** — culture fit matters
4. **Identify must-haves vs nice-to-haves**

### Research the Company
- Visit their website and about page
- Check recent news and blog posts
- Look at Glassdoor reviews
- Find employees on LinkedIn

## Phase 2: Customize Your Resume

### Create a Targeted Version

1. Open your main resume
2. Create a new version named for this application
   - Example: "Acme Corp - Software Engineer"
3. Keep the version synced initially

### Tailor Your Content

**Summary Section**
- Reference the company name
- Mirror their language
- Highlight your most relevant qualification

**Experience Section**
- Reorder bullets to prioritize relevant achievements
- Add keywords from the job posting
- Quantify impact where possible

**Skills Section**
- Move required skills to the top
- Add specific technologies mentioned
- Remove irrelevant skills

## Phase 3: Polish and Export

### Final Review Checklist

- [ ] Company/role name spelled correctly
- [ ] No typos or grammatical errors
- [ ] Contact information is current
- [ ] Length is appropriate (1-2 pages)
- [ ] Keywords from posting are included

### Export Your Resume

1. Review in the live preview
2. Export as PDF
3. Name the file professionally
   - `firstname-lastname-company-role.pdf`

## Phase 4: After Applying

### Track Your Application

Keep notes on:
- Date applied
- Version of resume used
- Job posting URL
- Any contacts at the company

### Prepare for Follow-up

- Set a reminder to follow up (1-2 weeks)
- Keep the version for reference
- Note any questions for the interview

## Pro Tips

### Timing Matters
- Apply early in the posting lifecycle
- Weekday mornings often have better visibility
- Avoid holiday periods if possible

### Customize the Right Amount
- Don't over-tailor (still be authentic)
- Don't under-tailor (show you read the posting)
- Balance personalization with professionalism
