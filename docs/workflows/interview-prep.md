# Using Your Resume in Interviews

Your resume is a conversation tool, not just an application document.

## Before the Interview

### Know Your Resume Cold

You should be able to:
- Discuss any bullet point in detail
- Provide specific examples for each achievement
- Explain the context and impact of your work

### Prepare STAR Stories

For each major experience entry, prepare:
- **S**ituation — What was the context?
- **T**ask — What were you responsible for?
- **A**ction — What specifically did you do?
- **R**esult — What was the outcome?

### Match Resume to Job Requirements

Create a mental map:

| Job Requirement | Resume Evidence | Talking Point |
|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| "Team leadership" | "Led team of 5" | The project story |
| "Problem solving" | "Reduced errors 40%" | The process improvement |
| "Communication" | "Presented to executives" | The stakeholder story |

## During the Interview

### Walking Through Your Resume

When asked "Tell me about yourself":
1. Start with your current role (brief)
2. Connect to 2-3 relevant highlights
3. End with why you're interested in this role

### When They Ask About Specifics

**"Tell me more about [this achievement]"**
- Expand with context they can't see on paper
- Use specific numbers and outcomes
- Connect to their needs

**"I see you worked at [company]"**
- Share relevant insights about that experience
- Highlight transferable learning
- Keep it positive

### Addressing Gaps or Concerns

If your resume has potential red flags:
- Prepare explanations in advance
- Keep them brief and positive
- Pivot to your strengths

## After the Interview

### Keep Track

Note which resume points generated:
- The most interest
- Follow-up questions
- Positive responses

Use this to improve future versions.

### Thank You Notes

Reference specific discussion points from your resume:
> "I enjoyed discussing my experience with [project]. I'm excited about bringing that same approach to [their challenge]."

## Pro Tips

### Print Copies
- Bring 3-5 printed copies to in-person interviews
- Use the same version you submitted
- High-quality paper makes an impression

### Portfolio Connection
If you have a portfolio:
- Reference relevant pieces during discussion
- "As you can see in my portfolio..."
- Have it ready to show
