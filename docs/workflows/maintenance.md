# Keeping Your Resume Fresh

Maintain your resume even when you're not job hunting.

## Why Maintain Your Resume?

- Opportunities come unexpectedly
- Details fade from memory over time
- Career progress is easier to track
- Reduces stress when you need to apply

## Regular Maintenance Schedule

### Monthly (5 minutes)

Quick check:
- Any new achievements to add?
- Any new skills learned?
- Contact info still current?

### Quarterly (15 minutes)

Deeper review:
- Update metrics with current numbers
- Add completed projects
- Review and refresh language
- Check for outdated information

### Annually (30 minutes)

Comprehensive update:
- Review entire resume structure
- Update summary for career progression
- Archive old, less relevant content
- Refresh template if needed

## What to Track Continuously

### Keep a "Brag Document"

Maintain a running list of:
- Projects completed
- Positive feedback received
- Metrics and achievements
- Skills learned
- Problems solved

When it's time to update, you have everything ready.

### Achievement Template

When something good happens, note:
```
Date: [When]
What: [What you did]
Impact: [Numbers, outcomes]
Quote: [Any praise received]
```

## Version Hygiene

### Clean Up Old Versions

- Archive versions you won't use again
- Delete truly obsolete ones
- Keep successful versions for reference

### Update Your Master

Your main resume should always be:
- Current with latest role
- Polished and error-free
- Ready to customize quickly

## Career Milestone Updates

### When You Get Promoted

- Update job title immediately
- Add achievements that led to promotion
- Adjust summary to reflect new level

### When You Complete Something Big

- Add it while details are fresh
- Include specific metrics
- Note technologies/skills used

### When You Learn Something New

- Add certifications right away
- Update skills section
- Note relevant training

## Pro Tips

### Set Calendar Reminders
- Monthly: "Quick resume check"
- Quarterly: "Resume deep review"
- Annually: "Comprehensive resume update"

### Keep It Private
Store your resume updates securely—don't update on work computers or during work hours if possible.
